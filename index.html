Aliases

Using Gmail Aliases (+tags)

Gmail lets you add a +tag after your username to create unique addresses for signups, filtering, and tracking. All emails still arrive in your main inbox!

Example:
username@gmail.com → username+facebook@gmail.com
Common Uses:
  • Separate signups
  • Spam tracking
  • Role-based aliases (e.g. support, jobs)
  • Easy filter/rule creation
Tips & Notes:
  • Enter a valid Gmail address (@gmail.com or @googlemail.com).
  • Usernames shorter than 2 characters may not work.
  • Some sites may block or strip plus-addresses—test if unsure.
  • Create Gmail filters for to:username+* to auto-label messages.

What is a Dot and Alias email address?

A dot email address is a variation of your email address created by adding dots (.) in the local part (before the @) to help organize and filter incoming messages. An alias email address is a forwarding address that redirects messages to your primary inbox without revealing it.

What are Dot and Alias email addresses used for?

Dot email addresses are used for unlimited sign-up options and organizing and filtering emails, while alias email addresses are used for privacy and to avoid spam.
